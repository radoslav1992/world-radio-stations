The United States has one of the largest and most varied radio landscapes in the world. Whether you want rolling news, public-radio journalism, classical music or the latest chart hits, there's a station for you — and most of them stream online for free. Below are some of the best-known American stations you can [listen to right in your browser](/).

## NPR (National Public Radio)

If you want serious journalism, [NPR](/stations/npr) is the obvious place to start. It isn't a single station but a network that distributes programs like *Morning Edition* and *All Things Considered* to more than a thousand local member stations. The tone is calm and explanatory, with reporting from correspondents around the world.

**Best for:** listeners who want news with context and depth.

## KEXP

Based in Seattle, [KEXP](/stations/kexp) is a listener-supported station that has become a global tastemaker for new and independent music. Its DJs choose their own playlists, and its filmed live in-studio sessions reach a worldwide audience online.

**Best for:** discovering new indie, electronic and alternative artists.

## KCRW

[KCRW](/stations/kcrw) in Santa Monica blends NPR news with one of the most influential music programs in the country, *Morning Becomes Eclectic*. Its music team has long shaped the soundtrack of Southern California's film and TV scene.

**Best for:** eclectic music fans who also want quality news.

## WNYC

[WNYC](/stations/wnyc) is the public radio voice of New York City and a major producer of national programs such as *Radiolab*. It runs complementary schedules on AM and FM and streams everything online.

**Best for:** news, talk and smart documentary-style shows.

## Z100

For pure pop, [Z100](/stations/z100-new-york) is New York's famous Top 40 station. It plays current chart hits and is known for big live events and high-energy morning shows.

**Best for:** chart music and pop hits.

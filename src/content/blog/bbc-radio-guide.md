The BBC operates one of the most respected public radio systems in the world, with several national stations that each serve a different audience. If you're new to British radio, here's how the best-known ones compare — all of them available to [stream online](/).

## BBC Radio 1 — new and chart music

[BBC Radio 1](/stations/bbc-radio-1) launched in 1967 as the BBC's answer to the pop pirate stations of the era. Today it targets a younger audience with current chart hits, dance and new music, plus live sessions and big events like the Radio 1 Big Weekend.

**Listen if you want:** the latest pop and dance, and emerging artists.

## BBC Radio 4 — speech and ideas

[BBC Radio 4](/stations/bbc-radio-4) is the corporation's flagship speech station. Instead of music, it offers news, current affairs, drama, comedy and documentaries. Programs like the morning news show *Today* are central to British public life.

**Listen if you want:** news, talk, drama and intelligent discussion.

## BBC World Service — global news

The [BBC World Service](/stations/bbc-world-service) began in 1932 and broadcasts news and analysis to a global audience in English and dozens of other languages. For many listeners around the world, it has long been a trusted source of international news.

**Listen if you want:** world news with a global perspective.

## Classic FM — accessible classical

Although it isn't a BBC station, [Classic FM](/stations/classic-fm-uk) deserves a mention as the UK's most popular classical station. Launched in 1992, it presents classical music — including film scores and popular favourites — in a relaxed, accessible way.

**Listen if you want:** classical music without the formality.

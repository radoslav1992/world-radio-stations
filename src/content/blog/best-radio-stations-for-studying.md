Music can be a powerful tool for concentration — but the wrong track at the wrong moment is a distraction. The trick is steady, low-attention sound that fills the silence without pulling your focus. Here's how to use radio for studying, deep work and coding.

## Start with a mood, not a station

Instead of hunting for the perfect station, start from the vibe. Our [Focus collection](/moods/focus) gathers calm, ambient stations from around the world — minimal vocals, steady textures, nothing jarring. For programming sessions, the [Coding & lo-fi collection](/moods/coding) leans into lo-fi beats and mellow electronic that keep you in flow.

**Best for:** instant, curated background sound with no setup.

## What to look for

The best focus stations share a few traits:

- **Few or no vocals** — lyrics compete for the language part of your brain.
- **Consistent energy** — no sudden jumps between quiet and loud.
- **Long-form flow** — ambient, lo-fi, classical and downtempo all work well.

You can find these by opening the [player](/#player) and tapping genre filters like **Chill**, **Electronic** or **Classical**, then saving the ones that work for you.

## Use the sleep timer and favourites

Set the player's sleep timer if you only want sound for a fixed study block, and star your best focus stations to your [favourites](/favorites) so you can start a session in one click. When you need a change of pace, the other [mood collections](/moods) are a tap away.

Find your focus, press play, and get to work.

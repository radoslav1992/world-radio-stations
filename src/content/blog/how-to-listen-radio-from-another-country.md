One of the best things about internet radio is that location no longer matters. You can listen to a station on the other side of the world as easily as your local one. Whether you're living abroad and missing home, learning a language, or simply curious about radio elsewhere, here's how to do it.

## Use a station directory with country filtering

The simplest approach is a player that lets you browse by country. In our [player](/#player), there's a country menu: pick a country and the list reloads with that country's most popular stations. Scroll down and more stations load automatically, so you can explore as deeply as you like.

This works for everything from the United States and the United Kingdom to Germany, France, Spain, Japan and beyond.

## Search by name if you know the station

If you already have a station in mind — say [BBC Radio 1](/stations/bbc-radio-1) or [France Inter](/stations/france-inter) — just type its name into the search box. The player searches the stations currently loaded for the country you've selected.

## Save your favourites

Found a station you love? Tap the star to add it to your [favourites](/favorites). They're stored locally in your browser, so they'll be waiting next time — handy if you regularly switch between, say, a station back home and one where you currently live.

## A few tips

- **Connect to Wi-Fi** when you can, to avoid using mobile data.
- **Try public broadcasters** for reliable streams: the BBC, NPR, Radio France and similar have robust, high-quality feeds.
- **Mind the time zone** — a "morning show" abroad might be airing in the middle of your night.

## Why it's worth it

Listening to radio from another country is a small but powerful way to feel connected — to home, to a language you're learning, or to a culture you're curious about. Open the [player](/#player), pick a country, and start exploring.

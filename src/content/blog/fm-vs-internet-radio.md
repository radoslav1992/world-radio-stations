Radio has been around for over a century, but the way we listen keeps changing. Today, FM radio and internet radio sit side by side. How do they actually differ, and which is better? It depends on what you value.

## How they work

**FM radio** transmits sound over the air using radio frequencies. A station's transmitter sends a signal that your receiver picks up through an antenna. It's local by nature — you can only receive stations within range of the transmitter.

**Internet radio** sends audio as a digital stream over the internet. Any connected device can play it, no matter where the station is located. (For a deeper look, see our article on [how internet radio works](/blog/how-internet-radio-works).)

## Choice

This is the biggest difference. On an FM dial you might find a few dozen stations. With internet radio you can reach **thousands** from around the world — every genre, every language, every country. Want jazz from New York or pop from Tokyo? It's a few clicks away in our [player](/#player).

## Sound quality

FM can sound excellent with a strong signal, but it's prone to interference, static and fading as you move. Internet streams are consistent as long as your connection is stable, though quality depends on the stream's bitrate.

## Cost and data

FM is completely free and uses no data. Internet radio is also usually free to listen to, but it uses mobile data unless you're on Wi-Fi — typically 60–120 MB per hour, depending on quality.

## Reliability

FM works without an internet connection, which makes it dependable in emergencies or remote areas. Internet radio needs a connection, but in return gives you metadata like song titles, station logos and the ability to save favourites and history.

## Which should you choose?

For local stations on the move with no data use, FM is hard to beat. For variety, global reach and extra features, internet radio wins easily. The good news is you don't have to choose — and with a [web player](/), the whole world of internet radio is already in your browser.

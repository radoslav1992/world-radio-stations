Public radio occupies a special place in American broadcasting. Funded largely by listeners, foundations and grants rather than advertising, public stations are free to focus on in-depth journalism and adventurous music. Here are some of the best, all of which [stream online for free](/).

## NPR and its member stations

[NPR](/stations/npr) is the backbone of American public radio. It produces national newsmagazines and distributes them to more than a thousand locally owned member stations. When you tune into many public stations across the country, you're hearing a blend of NPR programming and local content.

## KEXP — music discovery

Seattle's [KEXP](/stations/kexp) is proof that public radio can be a global music force. Its DJ-led programming and filmed live sessions have made it one of the most influential music stations anywhere, especially for indie and alternative artists.

## KCRW — eclectic and influential

[KCRW](/stations/kcrw) in Santa Monica pairs NPR news with a famously eclectic music identity. Its long-running show *Morning Becomes Eclectic* has helped break countless artists and shape the sound of film and television.

## WNYC — the public voice of New York

[WNYC](/stations/wnyc) serves the largest media market in the country and produces nationally syndicated shows like *Radiolab* and *On the Media*, combining local journalism with ambitious storytelling.

## Why public radio matters

Without the pressure of commercial ratings in the same way, public stations can take more risks — longer interviews, deeper reporting, and music you won't hear elsewhere. That independence is exactly what makes them worth seeking out.

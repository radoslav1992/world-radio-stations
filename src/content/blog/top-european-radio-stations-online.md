Europe's radio scene is wonderfully diverse: dozens of countries, dozens of languages and a strong tradition of public broadcasting alongside lively commercial stations. Here's a tour of some of the most notable European stations you can [stream online for free](/).

## United Kingdom

The BBC dominates British radio. [BBC Radio 1](/stations/bbc-radio-1) is the pop and chart station for younger listeners, while [BBC Radio 4](/stations/bbc-radio-4) is a speech network of news, drama and comedy that is admired around the world. The [BBC World Service](/stations/bbc-world-service) broadcasts news globally in English and many other languages. For classical music, [Classic FM](/stations/classic-fm-uk) offers an accessible, popular take on the genre.

## France

French public radio is exceptional. [France Inter](/stations/france-inter) is the leading generalist station, mixing news, culture and humour, while [FIP](/stations/fip) is a beloved, almost talk-free music station with famously eclectic taste. On the commercial side, [NRJ](/stations/nrj-france) is the country's biggest hit-music brand.

## Germany

In Germany, [Deutschlandfunk](/stations/deutschlandfunk) is a national news and information station with a serious, in-depth approach, while [1LIVE](/stations/1live-wdr) from broadcaster WDR is one of the country's most popular youth pop stations.

## Spain and Italy

Spain offers [Cadena SER](/stations/cadena-ser), a historic news-and-talk network, and [LOS40](/stations/los40), the country's leading chart station. In Italy, [Radio Deejay](/stations/radio-deejay) combines pop and dance with entertainment-driven shows.

## The Netherlands and Ireland

[NPO Radio 2](/stations/npo-radio-2) is a popular Dutch pop and rock station, famous for its year-end *Top 2000* countdown. In Ireland, [RTÉ Radio 1](/stations/rte-radio-1) is the flagship public station for news, talk and music.

## History

The station began in 1972 as KCMU, a small college radio outlet associated with the University of Washington. Over the following decades it built a reputation for adventurous, DJ-driven music programming. In 2001, following a partnership with the Experience Music Project, it relaunched as KEXP.

KEXP later became fully listener-supported under the non-profit Friends of KEXP, and in 2016 it moved into a purpose-built home at Seattle Center featuring public spaces and a live performance studio.

## Format and programming

KEXP is built around the idea of the curating DJ. Hosts choose their own music, spanning indie rock, electronic, hip-hop, world music and more, with a strong focus on new and emerging artists. The station is widely known for its live in-studio sessions, many of which are filmed and shared online, reaching a global audience.

## Reach

KEXP broadcasts on 90.3 FM in the Seattle area and streams worldwide via its website and apps. Its YouTube channel of live performances has extended its influence far beyond the Pacific Northwest.

## Significance

KEXP is one of the most respected music-focused public radio stations in the United States, often cited as a tastemaker for independent music and a model for combining terrestrial broadcasting with digital video and streaming.

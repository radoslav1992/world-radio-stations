## History

BBC Radio 1 launched on 30 September 1967, created as the BBC's response to the offshore "pirate" pop stations of the 1960s and the public appetite for contemporary music. Its debut helped reshape British radio, establishing a dedicated national outlet for pop.

## Format and programming

Radio 1 targets a younger audience with a playlist centred on current chart hits, dance and emerging artists. Its schedule mixes high-profile presenter shows with specialist programming, live sessions and events such as the Radio 1 Big Weekend. The station has expanded with spin-off services and a strong presence on the BBC Sounds platform and on video.

## Reach

Radio 1 broadcasts nationally across the United Kingdom on FM and DAB digital radio, and streams worldwide through BBC Sounds.

## Significance

As the BBC's principal pop station, Radio 1 has played a central role in British music culture for decades, influencing the charts and providing a platform for new artists.

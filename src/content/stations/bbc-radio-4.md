## History

BBC Radio 4 took its current name and identity in 1967, succeeding the earlier BBC Home Service. It became the corporation's principal speech network, carrying the kind of news, talk and spoken-word programming that had long anchored British public radio.

## Format and programming

Radio 4 is built around speech rather than music. Its schedule includes flagship news programs such as *Today*, alongside drama, comedy, documentaries, panel shows and long-running strands. Hourly news, in-depth current affairs and a tradition of original radio drama are central to its identity.

## Reach

The station broadcasts nationally on FM, on Long Wave (198 kHz) and on DAB digital radio, and streams worldwide via BBC Sounds.

## Significance

Radio 4 is one of the most influential speech radio stations in the world, widely regarded as a flagship of British public-service broadcasting and a key forum for national debate, culture and the arts.

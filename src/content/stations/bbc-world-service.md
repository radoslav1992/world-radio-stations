## History

The BBC World Service began in 1932 as the BBC Empire Service, broadcasting on shortwave to reach English-speaking listeners around the world. Over the following decades it expanded into many languages and became one of the most widely heard international broadcasters, particularly valued during periods when independent news was scarce in parts of the world.

## Format and programming

The English-language World Service focuses on international news, analysis and discussion, with rolling coverage of global events and programs exploring science, culture, business and sport. In addition to English, the World Service produces content in dozens of other languages for regional audiences.

## Reach

The World Service reaches listeners globally through online streaming, partner and rebroadcasting stations, and historically via shortwave. It is one of the BBC's most international-facing services.

## Significance

The BBC World Service is among the best-known international broadcasters in the world and a long-standing reference point for global news in English and many other languages.
